Past Research Projects

Investigating Bias in Machine Learning Models for Homelessness Resource Allocation

Davidson College, Texas Advanced Computing Center (TACC), The University of Texas at Austin
Abigail Santiago, Dr. Anne Bowen

This project sought to identify potential areas of bias in machine learning models that allocate resources for homelessness prevention and intervention by exploring the concepts of fairness and searching for signs of underspecification.

This project was accepted for presentation at the Practice and Experience in Advanced Research Computing (PEARC) for 2022, and was presented in July 2022. Check out the conference proceeding here, and read the extended abstract here!

This project was accepted for presentation at the Emerging Researchers National Conference in STEM (ERN) for 2022, which was later postponed due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

Why You Should Diversify Your Syllabus

Davidson College
Abigail Santiago, Benjamin Santiago, Sydney Finkelstein, Dr. Raghu Ramanujan

This project sought to explore the effects of only teaching material created by white authors. To do so, several Natural Language Processing Machine Learning models were created in order to measure and detect bias in language in published History papers.

Exploring Self-Teaching Game-Playing Algorithm

Davidson College

This project sough to compare the accuracy and efficiency between self-taught human-encoded chess programs.
